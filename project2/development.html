Film Processing:

Before film can be exposed to light to enlarge the image, it must be developed. The process is completed in just a few steps, however, these steps can also be lengthy. Developers should also be aware of the fragility of film when handling it; meaning film should not be exposed to light before the process is over and should be touched minimally to reduce dust and fingerprints.

To load the film:

  1. Remove the film from its spool and cut the leading end

  2. Load the film onto a clean and dry reel and place it in a clean and dry light-tight tank

Image Development:

A typical darkroom is equipped with enlargers, chemicals, and running water. An enlarger is an optical apparatus that projects light through the image of a negative onto a base, finely controls the focus, intensity and duration of light exposure.

The Chemicals:

Multiple chemicals are used in both film processing and image development and share the same or a similar role.

  • Photo/Film Developer: Makes the image appear

  • Stop Bath: Stops development process

  • Fixer: Makes the image permanent

  • Perma Wash: The fixer remover

  • Photo Flo: A wetting agent to minimize water marks or streaks
