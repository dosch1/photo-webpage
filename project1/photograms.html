A photogram is a photographic picture produced with photographic materials but without a camera.

These images are created by placing objects directly onto the photographic paper and then exposing it to light; the paper is then placed in the darkroom chemicals to make the image appear.
Opaque objects appear white in the final product while translucent objects emerge as gray.
